## Meal Prep Strategy

### Sunday Mega-Prep (2-3 hours)

**Quinoa** (600g dry ‚Üí ~1800g cooked):

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "I'm Still Hungry"

**Solutions**:
1. **Add more vegetables**: 200-300g extra vegetables = +60-90 kcal, very filling
2. **Increase protein slightly**: +20g protein = +100 kcal, highly satiating
3. **Check meal timing**: Are you going >5 hours between meals? Add snack
4. **Drink more water**: Often thirst feels like hunger
5. **Eat more slowly**: 20-minute minimum per meal
6. **Add volume**: Soup, salad, vegetables before main course

**Do NOT**:
- Add empty calories (crackers, chips)
- Increase fats significantly (easy to overshoot calories)
- Go below 2400 kcal on rest days or 2800 on training days

### "I'm Too Full"

**Solutions**:
1. **Distribute eating more evenly**: Smaller, more frequent meals
2. **Liquid calories**: Smoothie for breakfast instead of whole meal
3. **Less fibrous vegetables temporarily**: 600g instead of 800g
4. **Patience**: Stomach adjusts to high-fiber diet in 1-2 weeks

### "It's Too Much Cooking"

**Solutions**:
1. **Increase Simple Health Kitchen orders**: 4-5x per week instead of 2-3x
2. **Double batch cooking**: Cook once, eat twice (or three times)
3. **Focus on simple dinners**: Grilled protein + roasted vegetables + microwave sweet potato = 15 minutes
4. **Use frozen vegetables**: Pre-cut, just as nutritious, super fast
5. **Cook on weekends only**: Batch everything, reheat all week

**Minimal Cooking Plan**:
- Breakfast: Overnight oats (no cooking)
- Lunch: Simple Health Kitchen (no cooking)
- Snacks: Greek yogurt + fruit + nuts (no cooking)
- Dinner: Sunday batch = 7 servings, just reheat (cooking 1x per week)

### "I Don't Like ___ Food"

**Swap Principles**:
- **Don't like salmon?** Use any fish (cod, tilapia, tuna)
- **Don't like Brussels sprouts?** Use broccoli, green beans, asparagus
- **Don't like quinoa?** Use brown rice, buckwheat, barley
- **Don't like ground beef?** Use ground turkey for everything
- **Don't like Greek yogurt?** Use Skyr, cottage cheese (low-fat), or extra whey protein

**Key is maintaining**:
- Same protein content
- Similar fat content (especially saturated fat)
- Similar sodium content
- Similar preparation method

### "I Ate Off-Plan"

**Do NOT**:
- Skip next meal to "compensate"
- Over-exercise to "burn it off"
- Restrict severely the next day
- Give up on the whole plan

**DO**:
- Log it honestly (learn from it)
- Return to plan at very next meal (not tomorrow, NEXT MEAL)
- Drink extra water if high-sodium meal
- Reflect: What triggered it? How to prevent next time?
- Move on (one meal doesn't erase a week of good eating)

### "The Numbers Don't Match"

**If weekly tracking shows you're off target**:

1. **Check portion sizes**: Use food scale for 1 week to recalibrate eyeballing
2. **Check meat ratios**: Is package actually 93/7 or did you grab 85/15?
3. **Check hidden sodium**: Sauces, pre-seasoned items, cheese
4. **Check cooking oils**: Easy to add 2-3 tbsp without noticing = +30g fat
5. **Check snacking**: Small bites throughout day add up

**Recalibration week**:
- Weigh and measure EVERYTHING for 7 days
- Include cooking oils, condiments, nibbles
- Compare logged values to tracking tool
- Identify discrepancies

## Appendix B: Cooking Reference

### How to Cook Perfectly

#### Chicken Breast (Moist, Not Dry)
1. Pound to even thickness (or butterfly)
2. Season with herbs, garlic powder, paprika (no salt)
3. Grill: Medium-high heat, 6-7 min per side (internal temp 165¬∞F/74¬∞C)
4. Bake: 180¬∞C for 20-25 minutes (internal temp 165¬∞F/74¬∞C)
5. Rest 5 minutes before slicing
6. Store with splash of lemon juice or broth

#### Salmon (Flaky, Not Overcooked)
1. Pat dry, season with dill, lemon, black pepper
2. Bake: 180¬∞C for 12-15 minutes (internal temp 145¬∞F/63¬∞C)
3. Pan-sear: Medium heat, 4 min per side (skin-side first if skin on)
4. Should flake easily but still be slightly translucent in center

#### Lean Ground Beef (Flavorful, Not Bland)
1. Use 93/7 or 95/5 lean-to-fat ratio
2. Don't press down while cooking (loses moisture)
3. Season heavily with: garlic, onion, cumin, paprika, oregano, black pepper
4. Brown over medium-high heat in batches (don't crowd pan)
5. Add acid (tomatoes, lemon juice) to brighten flavor

#### Quinoa (Fluffy, Not Mushy)
1. Rinse very well under cold water (removes bitterness)
2. Use 2:1 water to quinoa ratio (e.g., 400ml water for 200g quinoa)
3. Bring to boil, reduce to low simmer, cover
4. Cook 15 minutes, don't lift lid
5. Remove from heat, let sit 5 minutes covered
6. Fluff with fork

#### Vegetables (Flavorful, Not Bland)
1. **Roasting** (best for: Brussels sprouts, broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini):
   - Cut uniformly
   - Toss with minimal oil (spray or 1 tsp per 200g veg)
   - Spread in single layer (don't crowd)
   - Roast 200¬∞C for 20-30 minutes
   - Season AFTER with lemon juice, herbs, garlic

2. **Steaming** (best for: broccoli, green beans, asparagus):
   - Steam 4-6 minutes until bright green, tender-crisp
   - Immediately season with lemon juice, garlic, herbs

3. **Stir-frying** (best for: bok choy, snap peas, bell peppers):
   - Very hot pan
   - Minimal oil (1 tsp sesame oil)
   - Cook in batches (don't crowd)
   - 3-5 minutes total
   - Season with coconut aminos, garlic, ginger

**Protein: 170-180g (2.0-2.1g/kg)**
- Research shows 1.6-2.2g/kg optimal for muscle preservation during body recomposition
- Higher end needed when in caloric deficit or maintenance
- Distributed across 4-5 meals = optimal muscle protein synthesis

**Calorie Targets**:
- Training days: 2900 kcal (small surplus supports training)
- Rest days: 2500 kcal (small deficit creates recomposition)
- Weekly average: ~2740 kcal (maintenance with slight deficit)

**Why this works**: Cycling calories based on training allows muscle growth on training days and fat loss on rest days, while high protein preserves lean mass.

### Cardiovascular Health Targets

**Sodium: <2300mg**
- WHO recommendation for blood pressure management
- DASH diet target
- Reduces fluid retention and blood pressure

**Potassium: 4000-4700mg**
- WHO recommendation for cardiovascular health
- Counteracts sodium effects on blood pressure
- Supports nerve and muscle function

**Na:K Molar Ratio <1.0**
- Strong predictor of cardiovascular events (better than sodium alone)
- Ratio of sodium ions to potassium ions (what your body responds to)
- This plan targets 0.7-0.9 ratio

**Saturated Fat: <20g (<7% of calories)**
- American Heart Association recommendation
- LDL cholesterol reduction
- CVD risk reduction

### Fiber: 40g+

**Benefits**:
- Blood sugar stabilization
- Cholesterol reduction (especially soluble fiber)
- Gut microbiome health
- Satiety and weight management
- Colon cancer prevention

**Evidence**: Higher fiber intakes (38-50g) associated with better health outcomes than minimum recommendations (25-30g).

### Micronutrient Completeness

This plan provides adequate amounts of all essential micronutrients through whole food sources:
- **B-vitamins**: Whole grains, lean meats, legumes
- **Vitamins A/C/E/K**: Colorful vegetables, berries
- **Vitamin D**: Salmon, fortified foods (may need supplement in winter)
- **Minerals**: Wide variety of plant and animal foods
- **Omega-3**: Salmon 3x/week provides EPA/DHA
